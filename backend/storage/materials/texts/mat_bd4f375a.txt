Partner Wallet System Documentation
1. Overview
The Partner Wallet System is the financial backbone of the Rerender platform, designed to manage vendor earnings, commissions, and payouts with absolute precision. It provides partners with a real-time ledger of their financial health, ensuring transparency in every transaction from order placement to final bank settlement.
The system utilizes a Double-Entry Ledger Architecture, where every economic event (order delivery, refund, withdrawal) creates balanced entries across internal accounts, preventing "ghost" money or data inconsistency.
2. System Objectives
Transparency: Partners can drill down into any balance to see the exact orders and maturation dates.
Accurate Earnings Tracking: Automated calculations based on SKU-level settlement prices and platform commission rates.
Settlement Management: A robust maturation workflow that handles the transition from "Pending" funds to "Available" cash after return windows close.
3. Complete Wallet Workflow
The lifecycle of funds within the system follows a strict, state-driven path:
Order Placement: Customer places an order (Prepaid or COD). No wallet impact yet.
Order Completion (Delivery): When an order status updates to delivered, the trg_process_order_financials_consolidated trigger executes:
Earnings Calculation: Determines Partner Payout vs. Platform Commission.
Money Holding Logic:
If Prepaid/Admin COD: Cash is held by the platform.
If Partner COD: Cash is held by the vendor.
Wallet Credit Calculation: Funds are recorded in the ledgers table. Partner's share enters the pending_balance.
Maturation Period: Funds stay "Pending" for a specified window (typically 7 days) based on the product's return/replacement policy.
Settlement Delay Logic: The age_partner_balances() function periodically checks for matured entries where the return window has closed and no active return requests exist.
Available Balance: Matured funds move from pending_balance to available_balance.
Withdrawal Request: Partner initiates a payout for any amount up to their available_balance.
Admin Approval: Platform administrator reviews the withdrawal request.
Final Payout: Upon approval, funds are transferred via bank transfer, and the wallet is debited.
4. Detailed Financial Logic
Pricing Definitions
MRP (Base Price): The maximum retail price of the product.
Selling Price: The actual price paid by the customer after discounts.
Settlement Price: The fixed price promised to the partner per SKU (defined in product_variants).

Money Holder Logic
The system dynamically handles who physically holds the cash to prevent unnecessary transfers:
Admin Holds Cash: If payment is prepaid or shipping_handler is admin.
Partner Holds Cash: If payment is cod AND shipping_handler is partner.

6. Transaction System (Ledger)
Every movement is recorded as a Ledger Entry.
Example: Prepaid Order (₹1,000 Selling, ₹800 Settlement)
Credit (Partner): ₹800 (Category: partner_earning, Status: Pending)
Credit (Admin): ₹200 (Category: platform_commission)
Example: Partner COD Order (₹1,000 Selling, ₹800 Settlement)
Credit (Admin): ₹200 (Category: platform_commission)
Debit (Partner): ₹200 (Category: commission_payable) -> Partner keeps ₹1000 cash, but owes ₹200 commission.

9. Business Rules
Settlement Cycle: T+7 days after delivery (configurable via product policy).
Minimum Withdrawal: (Customizable, defaults to ₹500).
Dues Clearing: If a partner has commission_payable > 0, any withdrawal request will deduct the dues first from the bank transfer.
Refund Policy: Refunds trigger a reverse ledger entry, debiting the partner's pending or commission account accordingly.
10. Security & Reliability
ACID Transactions: All financial updates are wrapped in Postgres transactions.
Idempotency: Prevent duplicate trigger execution via state checks (OLD.status != 'delivered').
Audit Logs: The ledgers table serves as a permanent, immutable audit trail.
Fraud Prevention: Manual payment reports for dues require Admin verification before the balance is updated.
11. UI/UX Explanation
Dashboard: Displays three high-impact Cards (Pending, Available, Commission Due) with "Drill-down" capability.
Drill-down Modal: Permits partners to see exactly which orders make up a specific balance.
Transaction History: Filterable table showing credits (earnings) and debits (withdrawals/refunds).
12. Example Scenarios
Scenario A: Normal Prepaid Order
Order Total: ₹2,000
Settlement: ₹1,600
On Delivery: pending_balance increases by ₹1,600.
At T+7 Days: pending_balance decreases by ₹1,600; available_balance increases by ₹1,600.
Scenario B: Returned Order (Prepaid)
Order has matured (₹1,600 in available_balance).
Customer returns product.
On Approval: Ledger entry debit for ₹1,600. available_balance reduced.
13. Edge Cases
Double Withdrawal: The request_withdrawal RPC checks for available_balance at the time of execution; database constraints prevent negative balances.
Negative Balance handling: Occurs if a refund happens but the partner has already withdrawn funds. This creates a "Debt" entry in the available_balance that is cleared by future earnings.
Component | Formula
Partner Earning | Settlement Price * Quantity (or Selling Price * 0.8 as fallback)
Platform Commission | Selling Price - Partner Earning
Commission Due (for COD) | Selling Price - Partner Earning (Owed by partner to platform)
Metric | Description
Pending Balance | Earnings from delivered orders still within the return/refund window.
Available Balance | Funds that have cleared the return window and are ready for withdrawal.
Commission Due | Funds owed by the partner to the platform (specifically for COD orders where the partner collected cash).
Total Earnings | Lifetime net earnings (cumulative matured and pending).
